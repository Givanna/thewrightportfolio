Why Project Management?

Leadership: A Journey That Started Young

Leadership has always been in my DNA. Growing up with a sister who has special needs, I took on a lot of responsibilities early on. From managing the household to caring for my younger sibling, while my parents focused on my sister. It wasn’t a burden—it was a privilege. These experiences taught me the importance of empathy and efficiency, skills I’ve been sharpening at every seminar and convention I can find.

The Thrill of Seeing a Project Through

I’ve always been the one to jump at the chance to lead a project. There's something incredibly satisfying about taking a project from start to finish. The joy and sense of accomplishment that comes from seeing the final product is what keeps me going. It’s like solving a puzzle where every piece falls into place perfectly, and I just can’t get enough of that feeling.

Facing Insecurities Head-On

When I started working, I had my share of insecurities that held me back from diving straight into project management. But as I navigated through different roles, I couldn’t help but notice all the ways things could be better—better leadership, more efficient processes, you name it. So, I decided to make as many changes as I could. Project management felt like the next natural step, a place where I could really make a difference.

Finding My Fit in Project Management

My path to project management hasn’t been straightforward, but it’s a path that feels right. I love leading, I thrive on seeing projects through, and I’m passionate about making things better. It’s the perfect fit for me, and I’m excited about where this journey will take me.

Join Me on This Adventure

I’m just getting started, and I’d love for you to follow along as I continue this adventure in project management. I’ll be sharing my ups, downs, and everything in between. Stick around to see where this road leads and how I keep growing in this awesome field.
